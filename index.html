Who are the Karen people?

The Karen are one of the largest ethnic minority groups in Burma, residing in the southeastern region.

Decades of persecution by the Burmese government upon the Karen and other ethnic minority groups, eventually led to civil war between the Burmese military and these indigenous peoples in 1948.

This is currently the world’s longest ongoing civil war. Thousands of Karen have been forced to take refuge on the Thai-Burmese border.

About 500,000 people remain in the refugee camps today.

What are we doing to help?

We believe that education should be a basic human right therefore, we are sponsoring the Mae Ra Moe Junior College (post-secondary school in a refugee camp on the Thai-Burmese border).

MRMJC has been supported by the Child’s Dream Foundation from 2008 to 2018 however, that support ended in 2019. This will have a significant impact on the funding of the school that we hope to mitigate.
